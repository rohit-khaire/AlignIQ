## **Password Complexity Requirements** 

Category: Authentication 

### **Business Objective** 

Authentication controls establish confidence that a party requesting access to a system is who they claim to be, forming the first line of defense against unauthorized account use. The Password Complexity Requirements policy applies this principle specifically to password complexity requirements, establishing a clear, auditable baseline that reduces the likelihood of a preventable security or compliance failure in this area. 

### **Policy Statement** 

All systems, personnel, and processes within scope of Authentication must comply with the requirements defined under Password Complexity Requirements, and exceptions must be formally documented and approved by Director of Identity & Access Management. 

### **Detailed Requirements** 

**1.** Credentials must never be transmitted or stored in plaintext. 

**2.** Authentication attempts must be rate-limited to reduce brute-force exposure. 

**3.** Default vendor credentials must be changed prior to production deployment. 

**4.** Authentication events must be logged with timestamp, source IP, and outcome. 

**5.** Session tokens issued after authentication must expire after a defined inactivity period. 

**6.** Authentication mechanisms must be reviewed at least annually for continued adequacy. 

**7.** Fallback or legacy authentication paths must be disabled unless formally approved. 

### **Indicators of Non-Compliance** 

- Use of shared login credentials across multiple employees 

- Authentication endpoints without rate limiting or lockout 

- Default or vendor-supplied credentials still active in production 

- Absence of authentication event logging 

### **Required Evidence** 

- Authentication system configuration exports 

- Identity provider configuration screenshots 

- Sample authentication logs covering a 30-day window 

- Password policy configuration from directory services 

- Account lockout configuration settings 

### **Recommended Remediation** 

To remediate a violation of Password Complexity Requirements, the accountable team should: (1) Reconfigure the affected authentication system to align with the documented policy baseline and validate the change in a staging environment before production rollout. (2) Disable non-compliant authentication paths and route all access through the approved identity provider. Remediation actions must be tracked to closure and verified by an independent reviewer before the associated finding is closed.

## **Role-Based Access Control Enforcement** 

Category: Authorization 

### **Business Objective** 

Authorization controls ensure that authenticated parties can only perform actions and access resources consistent with their assigned role, limiting the blast radius of any single compromised account. The Role-Based Access Control Enforcement policy applies this principle specifically to role-based access control enforcement, establishing a clear, auditable baseline that reduces the likelihood of a preventable security or compliance failure in this area. 

### **Policy Statement** 

All systems, personnel, and processes within scope of Authorization must comply with the requirements defined under Role-Based Access Control Enforcement, and exceptions must be formally documented and approved by Director of Identity & Access Management. 

### **Detailed Requirements** 

**1.** Authorization policies must be version-controlled and peer-reviewed before deployment. 

**2.** Default-deny must be the standard posture for all new resource types. 

**3.** Access rights must be assigned based on documented job function, not individual request. 

**4.** Authorization decisions must be enforced at the resource layer, not solely in the client application. 

**5.** Elevated privileges must require a documented, time-bound approval. 

### **Indicators of Non-Compliance** 

- Absence of periodic entitlement review records 

- Segregation-of-duties conflicts left unresolved 

- New resources deployed with default-allow permissions 

- Users retaining access rights inconsistent with their current role 

- Standing administrative privileges without expiration 

### **Required Evidence** 

- Role and permission matrices 

- Entitlement review sign-off records 

- Access approval tickets 

- Identity governance platform exports 

- Segregation-of-duties conflict reports 

- Authorization policy source files 

### **Recommended Remediation** 

To remediate a violation of Role-Based Access Control Enforcement, the accountable team should: (1) Move authorization enforcement to the server or API gateway layer. (2) Revoke inconsistent entitlements and re-provision access strictly according to the role-based access model. Remediation actions must be tracked to closure and verified by an independent reviewer before the associated finding is closed.

## **Control Evidence Repository Management** 

Category: Compliance Documentation 

### **Business Objective** 

Compliance documentation policies ensure that policies, procedures, and control evidence are consistently maintained, version-controlled, and accessible to support audits and internal governance. The Control Evidence Repository Management policy applies this principle specifically to control evidence repository management, establishing a clear, auditable baseline that reduces the likelihood of a preventable security or compliance failure in this area. 

### **Policy Statement** 

All systems, personnel, and processes within scope of Compliance Documentation must comply with the requirements defined under Control Evidence Repository Management, and exceptions must be formally documented and approved by Head of Governance, Risk & Compliance. 

### **Detailed Requirements** 

**1.** Policy version history must be retained to show the evolution of requirements. 

**2.** Control evidence must be stored in a centralized, access-controlled repository. 

**3.** Superseded policy versions must be clearly marked and archived, not deleted. 

**4.** Policy changes must be communicated to affected stakeholders upon approval. 

**5.** All policies must have a designated owner accountable for accuracy and review. 

**6.** Policies must be reviewed and re-approved on a defined cycle, at minimum annually. 

### **Indicators of Non-Compliance** 

- Policies with no assigned owner 

- Policies not reviewed within the defined cycle 

- No retained version history for a policy 

- Control evidence scattered across untracked personal drives 

### **Required Evidence** 

- Policy management system records 

- Policy review and approval history 

- Control evidence repository access logs 

- Stakeholder communication records for policy changes 

### **Recommended Remediation** 

To remediate a violation of Control Evidence Repository Management, the accountable team should: (1) Establish and enforce a recurring policy review and re-approval cycle. (2) Migrate control evidence into a centralized, access-controlled repository. Remediation actions must be tracked to closure and verified by an independent reviewer before the associated finding is closed.
